I HATE THAT BLASTED WALL

Published on: November 13, 1988

Intro:

Today I was reading some old letters Lucille wrote for me. I think it's about time I share a story about us.

Our Story

We met in grade school when we were both six years old. She had the most beautiful pigtails I had ever seen, and I asked her if I could make her a flower crown, not just because she was beautiful and I wanted to be her friend, but also because I had just learned how to make them and I wanted to show off my newfound knowledge. We became playmates and we would go out on hikes every weekend. I remember seeing her crouching by the creek and looking at her tree bark boat as it sailed away. Then as we got older, we would sneak out to go to a field and watch the stars while we read each other poems and drank the night away. I remember I would get so antsy the evenings we had something scheduled. I longed to be her bedsheets so I could smell just like her. She smelled so sweet and warm, even when we were sweaty on those sweltering summer afternons out at the park, she smelled like a culmination of dreams. Then, her father got offered a job on the other side and she left. I know it's not anyone's fault but I'm still so bitter about it. They took away my Lucille. I remember our goodbye. We had snuck out to the field one last time, and we danced under the moon's glow. It started raining, almost as if the moon wept for us, and we ran home drenched. I was caught coming back, but it was so worth it. We still keep in touch but it's not the same as having her in front of me.

Conclusion

I just want to see her again. I miss when I could see her everyday but that stupid wall is in the way.
